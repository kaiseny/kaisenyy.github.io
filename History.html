Overview

History

The Brave Little Abacus blended emo, indie rock, math rock, and electronic sounds. Although the band was not widely known during its active years, it later gained a strong cult following online.

2007–2008

Formation & Early Releases

The Brave Little Abacus formed in 2007 in Sandown, New Hampshire, led by Adam Demirjian alongside high school friends Andrew Ryan and Zach Kelly-Onett. The group, consisted of Demirjian on vocals and guitar, Ryan on bass, and Kelly-Onett on keyboards. Before this, they played together in their first band, a ska group Eggplant Danceoff.

Early rehearsals took place in basements and garages in the Sandown area, using video game consoles like the Sega Dreamcast and PlayStation to run pre-recorded drum tracks. Local performances followed in local basements and small venues, often featuring the same console setups for backing tracks.

In 2008, they self-released their debut EP Demo?, featuring raw, experimental tracks like “The Buffalo” and “El Capitano” That same year, they released a split EP with fellow New Hampshire artist Matt Aspinwall, showing more structured songwriting and expanding on their sound with tracks such as "(Yes, He Did Help Overthrow Fulgencio Batista, But,) Che Guevara Didn't Wear His Own T-Shirts" and "[untitled]".

2009-2010

Breakthrough

In 2009, The Brave Little Abacus self-released their debut album Masked Dancers: Concern In So Many Things You Forget Where You Are. The album featured progressive song structures, with long, drawn-out tracks incorporating chaotic emo and math rock elements, shifting time signatures, and layered instrumentation like keyboards and horns. Its lyrics focused on youth and anxiety. Stand out tracks include "I See It Too," "A Map of the Stars" and "Born Again So Many Times You Forget You Are."

The album, along with their earlier releases, helped grow their presence in the New England DIY scene. From 2009 to 2010, they toured the Northeast, playing basements, garages, and venues like The Middle East and Silent Barn, building a dedicated underground following.

On May 29, 2010, they released their second album, Just Got Back From the Discomfort—We’re Alright, expanding their sound with greater dynamics, richer keyboard work, and more intense, emotional songwriting. The album mixed elements like post-rock, xylophones, and samples from Malcolm in the Middle, creating a distinctive, genre-blending style. Notable tracks include "Pile! No Pile! Pile!,""Please Don't Cry, They Stopped Hours Ago" and "The Blah Blah Blahs"

2011-2012

Disbandment

Just Got Back from the Discomfort—We're Alright became their most recognized release and later gained major appreciation in online emo communities.

2012–Present

Post-Breakup

After disbanding in 2012, the band’s music continued to spread online, earning them a lasting cult following and influence in modern emo music.
