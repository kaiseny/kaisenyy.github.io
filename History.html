Overview

History

The Brave Little Abacus blended emo, indie rock, math rock, and electronic sounds. Although the band was not widely known during its active years, it later gained a strong cult following online.

2007–2008

Formation

The Brave Little Abacus formed in 2007 in Sandown, New Hampshire, led by Adam Demirjian alongside high school friends Andrew Ryan and Zach Kelly-Onett. The group, consisted of Demirjian on vocals and guitar, Ryan on bass, and Kelly-Onett on keyboards. Before this, they played together in their first band, a ska group Eggplant Danceoff. Early rehearsals took place in basements and garages in the Sandown area, using video game consoles like the Sega Dreamcast and PlayStation to run pre-recorded drum tracks. Local performances followed in local basements and small venues, often featuring the same console setups for backing tracks. In 2008, they self-released their debut Demo? EP, featuring raw, experimental tracks like “The Buffalo” and “El Capitano” That same year, they released a split EP with fellow New Hampshire artist Matt Aspinwall, showing more structured songwriting and expanding on their sound with tracks such as "(Yes, He Did Help Overthrow Fulgencio Batista, But,) Che Guevara Didn't Wear His Own T-Shirts" and "[untitled]".

2008–2009

Early Releases

Projects like Demo? and Masked Dancers showed their experimental style and helped define their underground identity.

2010

Breakthrough

Just Got Back from the Discomfort—We're Alright became their most recognized release and later gained major appreciation in online emo communities.

2012–Present

Post-Breakup

After disbanding in 2012, the band’s music continued to spread online, earning them a lasting cult following and influence in modern emo music.
